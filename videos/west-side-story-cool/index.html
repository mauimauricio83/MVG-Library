Cool

West Side Story

Director
J. Robbins
Year
1961
Category
Dance
Genre
Soundtrack

The "Cool" number was designed to be a "pressure cooker" for the characters. Jerome Robbins, who co-directed the film and handled the choreography, was notorious for his perfectionism. He pushed the dancers to the brink of exhaustion to get the raw, agitated energy you see on screen. The brilliance of this scene lies in its dynamics. In most musicals, dancers expand their bodies to fill the space. Here, the Jets are constantly hunching over, clenching fists, and turning inward. The famous finger snaps aren't just for rhythm; they are a nervous tick, a way to release the adrenaline that is slowly killing them. It remains the gold standard for how to tell a story through movement alone. Tucker Smith, who plays the character Ice (the one leading the song), was actually the understudy for Riff on Broadway. Jerome Robbins was actually fired from the film before production ended because his perfectionism (and the endless takes required for scenes like this) put the movie dangerously over budget. The low ceiling set required special lighting rigs to be hidden in the floor and walls, creating the dramatic "noir" shadows.
