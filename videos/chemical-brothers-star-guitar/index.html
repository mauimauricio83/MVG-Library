The Chemical Brothers' "Star Guitar" is a hypnotic visual puzzle where the landscape itself is the score. Directed by Michel Gondry, the video appears to be a simple shot from a train window. In reality, every object that passes—from buildings to telephone poles—is perfectly synchronized to a specific instrument in the song. This wasn't a lucky coincidence; Gondry created a "visual score" and then painstakingly built the landscape in post-production, timing every element to match the track's architecture. It’s a brilliant execution of a simple idea, allowing the viewer to literally see the structure of the music.
