Petra Collins physically maps the lyrics about abandoning a wedding for personal freedom: a claustrophobic, pitch-black urban opening representing the trap of the altar, a rescue by a group of brides, an escape on the back of a motorcycle driven by another femme character, and a real forest at daybreak where wide tracking shots of a white horse symbolize baptism into new life. The title translates to "big fire" in Sicilian, and the track moves between Spanish and the Sicilian dialect.
