TLC's "No Scrubs" is a perfect time capsule of the Y2K aesthetic, directed by Hype Williams at the peak of his influence. The video showcases his signature style, placing the group in a sleek, futuristic space station to visually separate them from the subject of the song. The most interesting layer, however, is how it reflects the group's real-life dynamics. Left Eye’s extended solo section, with its own distinct sets and aesthetic, was a deliberate choice that mirrored her public statements at the time about feeling creatively sidelined. It’s a fascinating look at how a group's internal politics can be translated directly onto the screen in one of their biggest hits.
